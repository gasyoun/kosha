Per-sense corpus attestation · नागदन्त layer · kosha concordance

Every polysemous headword's numbered PWG sense → the passages that attest it. The load-bearing witness is PWG's own <ls> locus per sense (the split thin glossaries drop); DCS corpus attestations are joined on top where a verse-locus or gloss overlap fires.
Wave-1 pilot: 500 polysemous headwords (see the pilot list). Try nāgadanta — sense 1a (tusk) carries its MBH locus, sense 1b (peg) its Pañcatantra loci. Full data: the dataset TSV; low-confidence rows: the review queue.
Trust block. Source dataset: sense_corpus_concordance.tsv, built by build_sense_corpus_concordance.py (H1455) from the H1456 PWG per-sense <ls> export, the reused dict↔corpus concordance, DCS 2026 (CC BY 4.0) and the canonical pwg_sources.py abbrev resolver. Assignment tiers:
